🎯 What is Trading?
📌 The Simple Definition
Trading is the act of buying and selling financial assets (stocks, crypto, currencies, commodities) with the goal of making a profit from price movements.

You buy something at a low price and sell it at a higher price. Or you "short sell" — meaning you profit when prices fall.
📈
Long Trade (BUY)
You believe price will go UP. You buy now, sell later at a higher price. Profit = difference.
📉
Short Trade (SELL)
You believe price will go DOWN. You sell now, buy back cheaper later. Profit = difference.
⏱️
Timeframes
Day trading (hours), Swing trading (days-weeks), Position trading (months-years).
🏛️ Who Moves the Markets?
PARTICIPANTWHO THEY AREIMPACT ON MARKET
🏦 Institutional TradersBanks, hedge funds, mutual fundsVery High — move prices
🤖 Algorithmic TradersAI bots executing thousands of trades/secVery High — 70% of volume
🏢 Market MakersProvide liquidity, always buying & sellingHigh
👤 Retail TradersIndividual traders like you and meLow individually
💡 KEY INSIGHT
As a retail trader, you are trading AGAINST algorithms and institutions with billions in capital. This is why strategy, discipline, and risk management matter more than just "picking the right stock."
💸 How is Money Made in Trading?
Profit = (Exit Price - Entry Price) × Number of Units Example: Buy 1 BTC at ₹60,00,000 Sell 1 BTC at ₹66,00,000 Profit = ₹6,00,000 (10% gain) ✅ Short Example: Sell 1 BTC at ₹60,00,000 Buy back at ₹54,00,000 Profit = ₹6,00,000 (10% gain on falling price) ✅

🕯️ Anatomy of a Candlestick
📌 What One Candle Tells You
Each candlestick represents a time period (1 min, 5 min, 1 hour, 1 day etc.) and shows 4 key prices:

🟢 Green Candle (Bullish) = Price went UP in that period
🔴 Red Candle (Bearish) = Price went DOWN in that period

Open = Price at the START of the period
Close = Price at the END of the period
High = Highest price reached
Low = Lowest price reached
Body = Thick part (Open to Close)
Wick/Shadow = Thin lines above and below the body
🔍 Key Candlestick Patterns
🔨
Hammer — Bullish Reversal
Small body at top, long lower wick. Means sellers tried to push price down but buyers fought back strongly. Found at bottoms. = BUY signal.
⭐
Doji — Indecision
Open and close are almost the same, creating a cross shape. Neither buyers nor sellers won. Market is pausing — watch for breakout direction.
🌟
Engulfing Pattern — Strong Signal
A large candle completely "engulfs" the previous candle. Bullish Engulfing (green) = strong buy. Bearish Engulfing (red) = strong sell.
🌠
Shooting Star — Bearish Reversal
Small body at bottom, long upper wick. Buyers tried to push price up but sellers dominated. Found at tops. = SELL signal.
3️⃣
Three White Soldiers / Three Black Crows
3 consecutive strong green candles = powerful bullish momentum. 3 red candles = powerful bearish. High reliability pattern.

SUPPORT & RESISTANCE
The foundation of technical analysis. Learn where prices bounce, break, and reverse.
INTERMEDIATE
15 MIN READ
🏗️ What Are They?
📌 Support Level
Support is a price level where the market has repeatedly bounced UP from. Think of it as a "floor." Buyers step in at this level because they believe the price is cheap. The more times price bounces off a level, the stronger that support is.
📌 Resistance Level
Resistance is a price level where the market has repeatedly been rejected DOWN from. Think of it as a "ceiling." Sellers dominate at this level. A strong resistance that breaks becomes the new support — this is called Role Reversal.
⚡ How to Trade S&R
1
Identify the Level
Look for areas where price has reversed at least 2-3 times. The more touches, the stronger the level.
2
Wait for Price to Return
Don't chase. Wait for price to come back to your identified level. Patience is key.
3
Look for Confirmation Candle
Wait for a bullish candle (at support) or bearish candle (at resistance) to confirm the bounce. Never enter blindly.
4
Enter with Stop Loss
Place Stop Loss just BELOW support (for buy) or ABOVE resistance (for sell). A few points buffer is smart.

RSI & MACD INDICATORS
The two most powerful and widely used indicators. Learn to read momentum and trend strength.
INTERMEDIATE
18 MIN READ
📊 RSI — Relative Strength Index
📌 What RSI Tells You
RSI measures how fast and how much price has moved. It ranges from 0 to 100.

🔴 Above 70 = OVERBOUGHT — price may be too high, possible reversal down
🟢 Below 30 = OVERSOLD — price may be too low, possible reversal up
⚪ 50 level = Neutral momentum

Divergence = When price makes new high but RSI doesn't = hidden weakness = potential reversal signal
RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS)) RS = Average Gain / Average Loss over 14 periods Simple Rules: • RSI > 70 → Consider SELL or exit longs • RSI < 30 → Consider BUY or exit shorts • RSI crossing 50 upward → Bullish momentum building • RSI crossing 50 downward → Bearish momentum building
📈 MACD — Moving Average Convergence Divergence
📌 What MACD Tells You
MACD shows trend direction and momentum changes. It has 3 components:

📏 MACD Line = 12-period EMA minus 26-period EMA
📏 Signal Line = 9-period EMA of MACD Line
📊 Histogram = Distance between MACD and Signal

🟢 MACD crosses ABOVE Signal = Bullish crossover = BUY signal
🔴 MACD crosses BELOW Signal = Bearish crossover = SELL signal
📊 Histogram growing = Momentum increasing in that direction
💡 COMBINING RSI + MACD = CONFLUENCE
Best signals come when BOTH agree. Example: RSI below 30 (oversold) AND MACD bullish crossover happening at the same time = STRONG buy signal with higher probability.

MODULE 2 · LESSON 6
CHART PATTERNS
Patterns repeat because human psychology repeats. Learn to spot them before the move happens.
🔷 Continuation Patterns
🔺
Ascending Triangle
Flat top resistance + rising lows. Buyers getting more aggressive. Usually breaks UP. Strong bullish signal.
🏳️
Bull Flag
Strong upward move (flagpole) followed by slight consolidation downward (flag). Breaks upward. BUY on breakout.
📐
Symmetrical Triangle
Converging trend lines — lower highs and higher lows. Breakout can go either way. Wait for confirmed break.
🔄 Reversal Patterns
🙃
Head & Shoulders
Three peaks — left shoulder, higher head, right shoulder. Classic bearish reversal. SELL when neckline breaks.
🏔️
Double Top
Price hits same resistance twice and fails. W-shape at top. Strong SELL signal on second rejection and break below support.
W
Double Bottom
Price hits same support twice and bounces. W-shape at bottom. Strong BUY signal. Entry on break above the "neck".
⚠️ NEVER TRADE PATTERNS IN ISOLATION
Always combine patterns with S&R levels, RSI, and MACD for confirmation. A pattern alone is just a possibility — confluence makes it a high-probability trade.

MODULE 3 · LESSON 7
STOP LOSS & TAKE PROFIT
The most important lesson. This module alone will save your trading account from blowing up.
🚨 READ THIS FIRST
90% of traders who blow their accounts do so because they didn't use Stop Losses. No matter how confident you are in a trade — ALWAYS set a Stop Loss. The market can always go against you.
🛑 Stop Loss
📌 What is a Stop Loss?
A Stop Loss (SL) is a pre-set price level where your trade automatically closes if the market goes against you. It's your protection against catastrophic loss.

Without SL: 1 bad trade can wipe out 10 good trades
With SL: Every loss is limited and manageable
📍
Where to Place Stop Loss
For BUY trades: Place SL just BELOW the support level or the last swing low. For SELL trades: Place SL just ABOVE resistance or last swing high. Give it a small buffer (0.5-1%) to avoid being stopped by noise.
%
The 1-2% Rule
Never risk more than 1-2% of your total trading capital on ONE trade. If you have ₹50,000, max loss per trade = ₹500-₹1000. This ensures you survive 50+ consecutive losses without blowing up.
🎯 Take Profit
📌 What is Take Profit?
Take Profit (TP) is your target price where you lock in profits. Most professional traders use multiple TP levels:

🎯 TP1 = First target (1:1.5 Risk:Reward) — take 50% profits here
🎯 TP2 = Second target (1:2 R:R) — take 30% profits here
🎯 TP3 = Third target (1:3 R:R) — let 20% ride for maximum gain
Risk:Reward Ratio Example: Entry: ₹100 Stop Loss: ₹95 (Risk = ₹5) TP1: ₹107.50 (Reward = ₹7.50) → R:R = 1:1.5 ✅ TP2: ₹110 (Reward = ₹10) → R:R = 1:2 ✅ TP3: ₹115 (Reward = ₹15) → R:R = 1:3 ✅ Even if you win only 40% of trades with 1:2 R:R: 10 trades × 40% win = 4 wins × ₹10 = +₹40 60% loss = 6 losses × ₹5 = -₹30 NET PROFIT = +₹10 ← You're PROFITABLE! 🎉

MODULE 3 · LESSON 8
POSITION SIZING
How much to put into each trade. The math that separates professionals from gamblers.
💰 What is Position Sizing?
📌 The Core Concept
Position sizing answers: "How many shares/units should I buy?"

Most beginners randomly decide — "I'll put ₹10,000 into this trade." This is WRONG.
Professionals calculate based on their risk tolerance and stop loss distance.
POSITION SIZE FORMULA: Position Size = (Account Risk ₹) ÷ (Stop Loss Distance ₹) Example: • Account = ₹1,00,000 • Risk per trade = 1% = ₹1,000 • Entry price = ₹500 • Stop Loss = ₹490 (distance = ₹10) Position Size = ₹1,000 ÷ ₹10 = 100 shares If SL hits: Loss = 100 × ₹10 = ₹1,000 = exactly 1% ✅ If TP hits (₹520): Profit = 100 × ₹20 = ₹2,000 = 2% ✅
✅ THE GOLDEN RULE
Never risk more than 1-2% per trade. With ₹50,000 capital and 1% risk = ₹500 max loss per trade. You can lose 50 trades IN A ROW and still have ₹25,000 left. This is how pros survive and thrive.
MODULE 4 · LESSON 9
BUILD YOUR STRATEGY
Put everything together. Create a systematic, rules-based strategy you can follow consistently.
🏗️ The Components of a Strategy
1
Market & Timeframe
What market will you trade? (BTC, NIFTY, Gold?) What timeframe? (1H chart for swing, 15m for day trading). Stick to ONE market and ONE timeframe when starting.
2
Entry Rules (Exact)
Be specific. "I will buy when: RSI is below 35, price is at key support, and a bullish engulfing candle forms." Vague rules = inconsistent results.
3
Stop Loss Rules
Where exactly does your SL go? "SL goes 1% below the support level." Write this down. Never change it mid-trade.
4
Take Profit Rules
"TP1 at next resistance (1:1.5 R:R). TP2 at 1:2 R:R. Move SL to breakeven after TP1 hit." Pre-defined, not emotional.
5
Backtest It First
Before trading live, go back on TradingView and test your rules on 50-100 historical trades. Calculate: Win rate %, Average R:R, Max drawdown. Only trade it live if numbers are positive.
📋 Example Beginner Strategy
🟢 The EMA + RSI Pullback Strategy
Market: BTC/USD or NIFTY50 · Timeframe: 1 Hour chart

Setup:
✅ Price is above 50 EMA (uptrend confirmed)
✅ Price pulls back to the 50 EMA line
✅ RSI drops to 40-50 range (not oversold, healthy pullback)
✅ Bullish candle forms at the 50 EMA level

Entry: Buy on close of bullish confirmation candle
Stop Loss: Below the low of the pullback candle
TP1: Previous high (1:1.5 R:R minimum)
TP2: Next major resistance (1:2.5 R:R)

Historical Win Rate: ~58-65% with proper execution

MODULE 4 · LESSON 10
TRADING PSYCHOLOGY
The hidden reason most traders fail. Your biggest enemy in trading is in the mirror.
🧠 The Mental Enemies
😱
Fear
Makes you exit winning trades too early, miss valid entry signals, or hesitate on obvious setups. Results in underperformance.
🤑
Greed
Makes you hold winners too long, over-trade, ignore your TP levels, or add to losing positions hoping they recover.
😤
Revenge Trading
After a loss, you immediately take another trade to "get it back." This emotional trading leads to even bigger losses.
🙈
FOMO
Fear Of Missing Out. You see a coin pump 50% and jump in at the top. Chasing price is one of the most common ways to lose money.
✅ How to Control Your Emotions
📋
Follow Your Rules — No Exceptions
Write your strategy rules on paper. Before every trade, check if it meets ALL criteria. If not — don't take it. Period.
📔
Keep a Trade Journal
Record EVERY trade: entry, exit, reason, emotion felt, result. Review weekly. Patterns in your behavior will become clear quickly.
⏸️
The 3-Loss Rule
If you lose 3 trades in a row — STOP trading for the day. Your mind is not in the right place. Come back tomorrow fresh.
🎯
Focus on Process, Not Money
Don't think "I need to make ₹10,000 today." Think "I need to execute my strategy perfectly today." Profits follow process.
🧘
Detach From Individual Trades
Every single trade is just one data point in hundreds. A loss means nothing. A win means nothing. Only the system over 100+ trades matters.
💡 THE GOLDEN TRUTH ABOUT TRADING
"The market doesn't care about your feelings, your bills, your dreams, or your losses. It will do exactly what it wants to do. The only thing you can control is how you react to it."

⚡ The Perfect AI Trading Workflow
1
Check Macro Events First
Visit TradingEconomics.com. Are there any Fed meetings, CPI releases, earnings reports today? Big events = avoid trading right before them.
2
Check Company/Asset News
Use Finviz (stocks), CoinDesk (crypto), Reuters (forex). Know the fundamental backdrop for what you're trading.
3
Screenshot Your Chart
On TradingView, set up your indicators (RSI, MACD, S&R levels). Take a clean screenshot showing the full setup.
4
Feed Chart + News to AI
Upload the chart to NEXUS AI (or ChatGPT/Claude). Add the latest news headline. Ask: "Given this chart and news, what is your analysis? Entry, SL, TP?"
5
Cross-Check With Your Own Analysis
Do YOU agree with what AI says? Does it match your strategy rules? If yes → consider the trade. If your analysis conflicts → SKIP the trade.
6
Execute With Proper Risk Management
Calculate position size. Set SL and TP orders immediately. Walk away and let the trade play out. Don't watch every candle.

YOUR TRADING ROADMAP
A realistic, month-by-month action plan to go from complete beginner to consistent trader.
🗺️ Month-by-Month Plan
1-2
Months 1-2: Foundation & Paper Trading
Complete this course. Open a paper trading account (TradingView or Sensibull). Practice with fake money. Study 1 chart per day. Keep a journal. Goal: understand your chosen strategy without losing real money.
3-4
Months 3-4: Small Real Money
Start with ₹5,000-₹10,000 real capital. Max 1% risk per trade (₹50-₹100 per trade). The goal is NOT profit — it's to experience emotions with real money and manage them. Log every trade.
5-6
Months 5-6: Review & Refine
Review your trade journal. What patterns emerge? Where do you lose most? Refine your strategy based on real data. Begin increasing position sizes slightly if win rate is above 50%.
7-12
Months 7-12: Scale Slowly
Gradually increase capital as you prove consistency. Aim for 3-5% monthly return — not 50%. Focus on consistency over big wins. Learn one new concept per month (Fibonacci, Volume, Options basics).
2Y
Year 2+: Compound & Grow
With a proven track record, consider increasing capital significantly. At 5% monthly return on ₹5 lakh = ₹25,000/month. This is where real income begins to build — after proving consistency first.
